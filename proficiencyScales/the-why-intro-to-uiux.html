Compelling Question:
"How does a designer use psychology and visual logic to make technology 'invisible' and guide users toward their goals without them having to think?"

Learning Targets

  • Technical: I can differentiate between UI (Visual Interface) and UX (Architecture/Feel) and apply User-Centered Design (UCD) principles.
  • Technical: I can utilize Visual Hierarchy (Size, Color) and scanning patterns (F-Pattern/Z-Pattern) to guide the user's eye.
  • Technical: I can apply color psychology (Red for urgency, Blue for trust) and the 60-30-10 rule to create balanced palettes.
  • Technical: I can implement Gestalt Principles (Proximity, Similarity) and Affordances to make interfaces intuitive.
  • Technical: I can reduce "Cognitive Load" and "Friction" through consistent navigation, effective microcopy, and system feedback.
