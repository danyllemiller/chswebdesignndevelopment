TL;DR: Chapter 3

1. Research (The "Why")

Qualitative finds the "Why" (Interviews/Pain Points). Quantitative finds the "How Many" (Surveys/Stats). Never build for yourself—build for the user.

2. Personas & Empathy

A User Persona is a fictional character representing your data. Use an Empathy Map (Says/Thinks/Does/Feels) to get inside their head and solve their specific frustrations.

3. Competition & Gaps

Use a SWOT analysis to find strengths/weaknesses. Use a Competitive Matrix to find "Blue Oceans"—feature gaps where no one else is currently providing a solution.

4. User Stories & MoSCoW

Define features as: "As a [user], I want to [action] so [goal]." Use MoSCoW to prioritize Must-haves for your MVP (Minimum Viable Product).

5. The ADDIE Workflow

The professional cycle: Analyze (User), Design (Blueprint), Develop (Code), Implement (Launch), and Evaluate (Data).

6. Sitemap & Wireframes

A Sitemap is the map of pages. A Wireframe is the B&W blueprint of a single page's layout. We ignore color to focus on hierarchy and function.

7. Atomic Design & Reading

Build UIs using Atoms (Tags), Molecules (Groups), and Organisms (Sections). Position content based on F-Patterns (Text) or Z-Patterns (Visuals).

8. The SEO Trinity

Optimize for Google using the Title Tag, Meta Description, and a single H1. Use Long-Tail Keywords to capture specific user intent.

GUILD RULE: If it isn't in the wireframe, it shouldn't be in the code. Plan first, build second.
