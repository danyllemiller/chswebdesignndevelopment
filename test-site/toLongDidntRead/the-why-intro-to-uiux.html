The TL;DR: What You Really Need to Know

Core Concepts of UI/UX

  • UI (User Interface) is the look (colors, fonts, buttons).
  • UX (User Experience) is the feel (is it easy to use? is it fast?).
  • Good UX is more important than good UI. Remember the Squeezy Ketchup Bottle.
  • Accessibility (a11y) means designing for everyone. Always check your contrast and touch targets!
  • Mobile-First: Design for the small phone screen before you design for the big desktop screen.
  • A good site is clear , consistent , and gives feedback .
  • Wireframes (low-fi) are black-and-white blueprints. Mockups (hi-fi) are full-color, realistic pictures of the final site.
  • Use Figma to build your mockups before you start coding.
