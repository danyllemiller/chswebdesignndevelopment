THE ARCHITECT'S BLUEPRINT

Chapter 3: UX Research, Information Architecture & SEO

1. Research: Finding the "Who"

Qualitative (The Why)

Focuses on User Interviews and observation. We identify Pain Points (frustrations) and emotional motivations.

Quantitative (The What)

Focuses on Surveys and analytics. Gathers statistical data (percentages) to prove patterns across large groups.

The Synthesis: User Personas

We combine research into a fictional archetype to represent our real users. Use an Empathy Map (Says, Thinks, Does, Feels) to step into their shoes.

2. Strategic Analysis

SWOT Report
  • Strengths (Internal)
  • Weaknesses (Internal)
  • Opportunities (External)
  • Threats (External)
Blue Ocean Strategy

Use a Competitive Matrix to cross-reference features. Look for the "Blue Ocean"—the gaps where competitors are failing, allowing you to offer unique value.

3. The Agile Workflow

User Stories: The 3 C's

"As a [user], I want to [action] so that [goal]."

  • Card: Keep the requirement short.
  • Conversation: Discuss the details with the team.
  • Confirmation: Define Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then).

4. Structural Engineering

The Sitemap (The Map)

A top-down hierarchy of every file in the project. Always starts with index.html at the root level.

Wireframes (The Blueprint)

Black & White skeletal layouts focusing only on Hierarchy and Priority. We ignore color to focus on user journey.

Atoms

Basic HTML tags (Buttons, Inputs)

Molecules

Small groups (Search Forms)

Organisms

Sections (Site Header)

6. The SEO Trinity

  1. The Title Tag: The blue link in search results.
  2. Meta Description: The ad snippet under the link.
  3. The H1 Tag: The one main visible headline.
