Course Overview

This course is designed as a work-at-your-own-pace environment. This means you have the flexibility to move through the modules as quickly as you are able, allowing you to manage your own time and learning style.

⏰ Daily Attendance

While the coursework is flexible, our daily routine is not. Clock-ins are required every day before the first 5 minutes of class have passed.

🚀 Testing Out Early

Because this is a self-paced class, you have the unique opportunity to "test out" of the semester's work. You can take the final exam whenever you feel ready.

The Strict Rules

Be Here: You must take the test in my classroom.

Use your Chromebook: No other devices are permitted.

⚠️ Warning: If you fail to follow these rules—especially if you attempt to take the test outside the classroom—your attempt will not count, and you will automatically be required to finish all normal classwork for the semester.

The Outcomes
  • 80% or better: Congratulations! You are excused from all future assignments for this semester.
  • 75% - 79%: You may retake the exam one time to improve your score.
  • Below 75% (or failed retake): You must complete all remaining assignments and retake the final exam on the regularly scheduled day at the end of the semester.

Course Objectives

1 Foundations

Understanding the history and evolution of computing devices and data representation.

2 Computational Logic

Explaining key concepts including algorithms, abstraction, and data representation.

3 Programming Proficiency

Demonstrating skills with programming languages and software development tools.

4 Ethical Impact

Researching the relationship between computer science, society, and ethical responsibilities.

5 Cybersecurity

Understanding principles of digital safety and network defense.
