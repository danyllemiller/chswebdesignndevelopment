1.1 Digital Identity & Reputation

Your digital footprint is the permanent, searchable record of your online presence. In the modern workforce, this acts as your "second resume"[cite: 2, 6].

Case Law: Mahanoy v. B.L. (2021)

The Supreme Court clarified that while students possess free speech, schools can discipline off-campus behavior if it creates "substantial disruption." Your digital "venting" has real-world consequences[cite: 2].

1.2 Cyber Hygiene & Social Engineering

Hackers use social engineering to bypass technical defenses by manipulating human psychology[cite: 8].

  • Phishing: Scammers use "urgency traps" to make you bypass critical thinking[cite: 7, 8].
  • Encryption (HTTPS): Always check for the lock icon. Encryption scrambles data so it remains unreadable if intercepted on public Wi-Fi[cite: 10].
  • MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication ensures a stolen password isn't enough to compromise your account[cite: 10].

1.3 Intellectual Property

Respecting intellectual property (IP) is a core ethical standard[cite: 9].

  • Copyright: Automatically protects creative work upon creation[cite: 7].
  • Fair Use: Using the 4-Factor Test (Purpose, Nature, Amount, Market Effect) to determine if you can use media without explicit permission[cite: 9].
