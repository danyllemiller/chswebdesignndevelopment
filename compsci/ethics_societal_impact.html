Unit 5: Impacts of Computing

Chapter 13: Ethics & Societal Impact

Examining the moral responsibilities of technologists and technology's influence on society.

In this chapter: 13.1 The Ethics of Technology 13.2 Technology and Social Change 13.3 Responsible Innovation

As technologists, we have immense power to shape how society functions. With that power comes the responsibility to consider the ethical implications of our creations—and to ensure technology serves the greater good.

13.1 The Ethics of Technology Standard 9-12.IC.C.1

Technology does not exist in a vacuum. Every app, website, and algorithm is built by humans, for humans. Because of this, technology reflects the culture that creates it—including its flaws.

13.1.1 Ethical Frameworks in Tech

When making technology decisions, consider these ethical frameworks:

  • Utilitarianism: Does the technology create the greatest good for the greatest number?
  • Deontological Ethics: Are there moral duties or rules that should never be violated, regardless of outcomes?
  • Virtue Ethics: What would a "good" technologist do in this situation?

13.1.2 Code of Ethics

Professional organizations like the ACM and IEEE have established codes of ethics that technologists should follow:

  • Public: Technologists should prioritize the public good.
  • Client and Employer: Serve the legitimate interests of clients and employers.
  • Product: Ensure products meet the highest professional standards.
  • Judgment: Maintain independence and objectivity in professional judgment.
  • Professionalism: Uphold the integrity and reputation of the profession.

13.2 Technology and Social Change Standard 9-12.IC.C.2

Technology has the power to transform society—for better or worse. Understanding these impacts helps us build technology that benefits humanity.

13.2.1 Positive Social Impacts

Technology has created remarkable positive changes:

  • Democratization of Information: Anyone with internet access can learn about any topic.
  • Global Connectivity: People can communicate across continents instantly.
  • Healthcare Advances: Technology enables better diagnosis, treatment, and research.
  • Economic Opportunity: New industries and job categories have emerged.

13.2.2 Negative Social Impacts

Technology also brings challenges that society must address:

  • Mental Health Concerns: Social media use has been linked to anxiety and depression, especially in young people.
  • Misinformation: False information spreads rapidly online.
  • Privacy Erosion: Constant data collection raises surveillance concerns.
  • Economic Disruption: Automation can displace workers from certain industries.

13.2.3 The Digital Divide

We must ensure technology benefits everyone, not just those with access:

The Digital Divide is the gap between those who have access to technology and those who don't. Factors include:

  • Economic: Ability to afford devices and internet access.
  • Geographic: Rural areas often lack broadband infrastructure.
  • Educational: Digital literacy skills are not universal.
  • Disability: Not all technology is accessible to people with disabilities.

13.3 Responsible Innovation Standard 9-12.IC.C.3

responsible innovators anticipate potential harms and design technology that minimizes negative impacts.

13.3.1 Ethical Design Practices

Follow these principles when building technology:

  • Privacy by Design: Build privacy protections into products from the start.
  • Security by Design: Prioritize security in all product decisions.
  • Accessibility by Design: Ensure products work for all users, including those with disabilities.
  • Inclusive by Design: Consider diverse users and use cases.

13.3.2 Responsible AI Development

Artificial Intelligence requires special ethical consideration:

  • Transparency: Be clear about how AI makes decisions.
  • Fairness: Ensure AI doesn't perpetuate bias.
  • Accountability: Someone must be responsible for AI outcomes.
  • Human Control:Humans should remain in control of AI systems.

13.3.3 Future-Oriented Thinking

Consider the long-term implications of technology:

  • Sustainability: What is the environmental impact?
  • Interdependence: How does this technology affect other systems?
  • Reversibility: Can effects be undone if problems arise?
  • Stakeholder Involvement: Include affected communities in decisions.
