🎯 Essential Question:

How do ethical standards, web law, and accessibility guidelines ensure a safe and inclusive internet for everyone?

PART 1: Intellectual Property & Web Law
  1. Protecting the Mind's Work: Everything created with the mind is considered Property.
    • • : Automatic legal protection for original creative works (like code, photos, or text).
    • • Trademark: Protects brand identity (like logos or slogans).
    • • Patent: Protects new inventions or physical processes.
  2. Ethical Sourcing: You cannot just right-click and save any image from Google for a client. To share work legally, we use licenses, or we use work from the (where copyright has expired and is free for anyone to use).
  3. Corporate Secrets: An NDA stands for Non- Agreement. It is a legal contract professionals sign to protect a company's (proprietary info like code logic or client lists).
PART 2: Designing for All (Accessibility)
  1. The Curb Cut Effect: This is the sociological principle that designing for users (like building a wheelchair ramp on a sidewalk) inherently makes the product better for (like people with strollers or luggage).
  2. Accessibility Laws & Standards:
    • • ADA: The Americans with Disabilities Act. In the famous Domino's Pizza lawsuit, the Supreme Court ruled that websites are public accommodations and be accessible.
    • • WCAG: Web Content Guidelines. This is the international rulebook for making sure sites work for screen readers and keyboard-only users.
  3. Technical Accessibility Markers:
    • • Alt-Text: The alt attribute is mandatory for images. It provides a text description of the image for used by the visually impaired.
    • • Semantic Tags: Using tags like <h1> and <nav> instead of <div> tags so that machines can understand the structure of the page.
    • • Color : Ensuring there is a high enough mathematical difference in brightness between text and its background so it is readable.
PART 3: Digital Ethics & UI Manipulation
  1. Probity: In professional development, Probity means strict adherence to the highest principles of conduct, honesty, and integrity.
  2. Dark Patterns: Dark Patterns are deceptive User Interface (UI) tricks meant to users into taking actions they didn't intend to take.
  3. Types of Dark Patterns:
    • • Confirmshaming: Making the user feel for clicking the "No" or "Opt-Out" option.
    • • Roach Motel: A subscription or service that is incredibly easy to get into, but almost to cancel or get out of.
