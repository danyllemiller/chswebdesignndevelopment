How Do GPS Satellites Work?

It feels like magic, but the Global Positioning System is a symphony of physics and engineering. It relies on a constellation of satellites, each broadcasting a precise signal. Your phone's receiver triangulates your position based on the tiny time differences in receiving those signals, a feat made possible by Einstein's theory of relativity.
