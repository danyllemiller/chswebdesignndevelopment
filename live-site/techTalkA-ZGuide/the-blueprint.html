Tech Talk: Chapter 3

Exhaustive Vocabulary for UX Research, Architecture & SEO

Acceptance Criteria (AC)
The specific, imperative set of conditions that must be met for a User Story to be considered complete, typically written in a Given/When/Then format to ensure precision.
ADDIE Model
A systematic project management paradigm consisting of five iterative phases: Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, and Evaluate.
Atomic Design
A modular methodology for building design systems by breaking interfaces into their most fundamental components (Atoms, Molecules, Organisms).
Blue Ocean Strategy
The pragmatic design approach of identifying "uncontested market space" by offering features that competitors currently ignore.
Cognitive Load
The amount of mental effort required for a user to process information; professional architects aim to minimize this through simplicity and white space.
Competitive Matrix
An analytical visual comparison tool used to cross-reference feature availability between a product and its industry rivals.
Empathy Map
A collaborative tool used to articulate what a developer knows about a user, mapping their sensory experience to foster deeper altruism in design.
F-Pattern
The standard optical scanning phenomenon observed in text-heavy interfaces where users prioritize the top and left-most content.
Fidelity (Low vs. High)
The level of accuracy and detail in a blueprint; ranging from abstract sketches (Lo-Fi) to pixel-perfect mockups (Hi-Fi).
Information Architecture (IA)
The structural design of shared information environments; the blueprint that organizes content hierarchy (Sitemaps).
MoSCoW Method
A prioritization technique used to manage project scope by categorizing features as Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, or Won't-have.
Qualitative vs. Quantitative
The dichotomy between research that seeks "the why" through emotional insights (Qual) vs. research that seeks "the what" through measurable data (Quan).
SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
The strategic refinement of a site's structure and metadata to ensure maximum visibility on algorithmic search engines.
Sitemap
A hierarchical diagram representing the logical organization of all pages within a digital ecosystem.
User Persona
A fictional archetype synthesized from real research data used to represent a specific segment of the target audience.
Wireframe
A skeletal visual representation of a single page's interface, purposefully devoid of color and styling to prioritize layout efficiency.
