Tech Talk: Chapter 3

Exhaustive Vocabulary for Web Law, Ethics & Accessibility

Accessibility (a11y)
The professional and legal practice of making websites usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. The '11' represents the eleven letters between 'a' and 'y'.
ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)
The primary US civil rights law that makes web accessibility a legal requirement for public businesses and organizations.
Algorithmic Bias
When human prejudices or flawed data sneak into code, causing AI or software to produce systematically prejudiced or exclusive results.
Alt Text
Short for 'Alternative Text'. A mandatory attribute on <img> tags used by screen readers to describe visual content to blind users.
ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications)
A set of specialized HTML attributes that provide extra information to assistive technologies when standard tags aren't enough.
Asymmetric Encryption
A security method using a pair of keys: a Public Key (to scramble data) and a Private Key (to unscramble it). This is the core of the HTTPS handshake.
Attribution (BY)
A Creative Commons requirement stating that users must give proper credit to the original creator of a work.
Certificate Authority (CA)
A trusted third party (like a digital notary) that verifies a website's identity and issues the SSL/TLS security certificates.
Color Contrast Ratio
The difference in brightness between text and its background. Most legal standards require a minimum ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text.
Copyright ©
An automatic legal shield that protects original creative works (code, photos, text) the moment they are created.
Creative Commons (CC)
A system of flexible licenses that lets creators share their work with specific rules (BY, NC, ND, SA) rather than 'All Rights Reserved'.
Curb Cut Effect
The design principle stating that building for marginalized users (e.g., the disabled) ultimately creates a better experience for everyone.
Dark Pattern
A deceptive or manipulative user interface trick designed to confuse users into making choices they didn't intend (e.g., a hidden 'unsubscribe' link).
Digital Footprint
The permanent, searchable trail of data, searches, and activity you leave behind every time you use the internet.
Fair Use
A limited legal defense (not a right) for using small parts of copyrighted material for educational, news, or parody purposes.
GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
Europe's strict privacy law that uses an Opt-In model, requiring specific consent before any personal data is collected.
Hashing
A one-way mathematical process that turns a password into a scrambled fingerprint. It is more secure than encryption because it cannot be reversed.
HTTP / HTTPS
HTTP is like a Postcard (unsecured). HTTPS is like a Sealed Armored Letter (Secure/Encrypted) and is the modern professional standard.
Infringement
The unauthorized or illegal use of someone else's intellectual property (e.g., using a trademarked logo without permission).
Intellectual Property (IP)
The broad umbrella term for creations of the mind, legally protected by Copyright, Trademarks, and Patents.
Invasion of Privacy
The unethical act of collecting, sharing, or selling a user's personal data (PII) without their clear consent.
NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
A legally binding contract that creates a 'confidential relationship' to protect a company's sensitive trade secrets.
Opt-In vs. Opt-Out
Opt-In: Data collection is OFF by default (GDPR). Opt-Out: Data collection is ON until the user stops it (Nevada SB220).
Patent ℗
A legal protection for an invention or unique process. It is NOT automatic, must be granted by the government, and lasts about 20 years.
PII (Personally Identifiable Information)
Information that can be used on its own or with other data to identify, contact, or locate a specific person.
POUR Principles
The four pillars of accessibility: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust.
Public Domain
A 'free buffet' of creative works where the copyright has expired or been waived. Free for anyone to use for any reason.
Salting
Adding random characters to a password before hashing it to ensure identical passwords generate unique hashes, stopping 'Rainbow Table' attacks.
SSL/TLS Handshake
The 8-step secure greeting process where a browser and server verify IDs and share secret keys to start an HTTPS session.
Survival Clause
A section of an NDA stating that the duty to keep secrets continues even after an employee leaves a company.
Trademark ™
A legal shield that protects Brand Identity (names, logos, slogans) to prevent market confusion.
Trade Secrets
Proprietary information (like code logic or algorithms) that give a business its competitive edge. They are protected indefinitely via NDAs.
WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
The global technical standard for web accessibility. Most professionals target Level AA compliance.
Work Made for Hire
A legal contract where your employer owns the copyright to everything you build while working for them on the clock.
