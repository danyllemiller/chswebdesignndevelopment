Chapter 12: The "Manager"

Content Management Systems (CMS) & Platforms

Agency Scaling: Stop Coding Everything from Scratch

You know how to code. You can build a beautiful, responsive, interactive website from a blank text document. But what if the Carson High Web Design Agency gets hired to run a news website like IGN or the Nevada Appeal? They publish 100 new articles every single day. Do you think a web developer is manually opening an index.html file, typing out <p> tags, and pushing to GitHub 100 times a day? Absolutely not.

To maximize our agency's profit margins and empower our clients to make simple updates, we use a CMS. What does CMS stand for? It stands for Content Management System. It is a "system" designed to "manage" website "content" without needing to touch the code.

This changes your job. What is the primary difference between a builder and a manager in web design? Builders code from scratch; managers use a pre-built CMS to post content quickly. As an agency developer, your job is no longer to write the articles. Your job is to set up the CMS architecture so the marketing team, writers, and business owners can log in and manage the site themselves safely.

The Matrix: Pitching Platforms to Clients

How can you tell if a piece of software is truly a CMS? It separates the content creation from the code structure and design. This means your articles are stored in a database, and your design is stored in a Theme. If a Theme is the "clothes" of a website, what is the database? The brain/memory that remembers the content.

Because they are separate, what happens to your blog posts if you switch to a completely different WordPress Theme? The design changes, but the text and posts remain perfectly intact! Can you switch WordPress Themes without losing your Media Library uploads? Yes! The Media Library is the digital warehouse where all uploaded photos and videos are stored separately.

The 3 Tiers of Web Creation
Platform Type Examples Pros & Cons
"No-Code" Builders Wix, Squarespace Pro: What is the primary advantage of a 'No-Code' builder like Squarespace compared to a CMS? They are easier and faster for absolute beginners with no technical knowledge to set up.
Con: What is the primary disadvantage of a 'No-Code' builder like Wix? Vendor lock-in: you do not own the code and cannot easily move your site to a different hosting provider.
SaaS CMS (Rental) WordPress.com, Shopify Which platform represents the 'Rental' model where the hosting company manages everything for you? WordPress.com. You pay for convenience and hosting, but customization is limited.
Self-Hosted CMS (Owner) WordPress.org, Drupal Which platform represents the 'Owner' model where you download free software and host it yourself? WordPress.org. Why is WordPress.org the professional standard over website builders like Wix? Because of total freedom and ownership of every line of code.
The King of the Web: WordPress powers over 40% of the entire internet. What does it mean that WordPress is 'Open-Source'? The source code is free and available for anyone to inspect, modify, and enhance. What programming language powers the logic and templates of WordPress? PHP.

Note: Which of the following is NOT a popular Content Management System? Photoshop! That is a photo editor, not a website manager!

Information Architecture & The Dashboard

How do you typically access the hidden back-end login screen of a standard WordPress site? By Adding /wp-admin to the URL. Once logged in, you enter the Dashboard. What is the WordPress 'Dashboard'? It is the private back-end mission control for the website.

Content Types: Pages vs. Posts

A professional agency developer never confuses a Page with a Post.

  • Pages: What is a WordPress 'Page'? Timeless, permanent information that doesn't expire or belong on a news feed. If you want to create a 'Contact Us' section that lives in your main menu, should you use a Post or a Page? A Page.
  • Posts: What is a WordPress 'Post'? A timely, reverse-chronological news article or blog update. If you want to announce a weekend sale that expires on Sunday, should you use a Post or a Page? A Post.

Which piece of content relies heavily on Categories and Tags for searching? Posts. Categories are for broad topics (e.g., "News", "Events"), while Tags are for specific, detailed keywords. Because there can be hundreds of posts, they need organization labels.

What is the difference between a 'Draft' and a 'Published' post? Drafts are saved in the back-end but hidden from the public; Published posts are live.

Settings & Permalinks

Settings are crucial for professional decorum. When you change the 'Site Title' in WordPress General Settings, what happens? The browser tab and site header update instantly across the entire site.

What is the purpose of the 'Permalink' setting? To control the structure of the URL (e.g., making it `/about-us` instead of `/?p=123`) for better SEO and readability. You should always set Permalinks to "Post Name" before creating content!

Which setting sets a static page instead of blog posts on the front page? Settings > Reading.

Pro Tip: What does the 'Screen Options' tab do in the Dashboard? It allows you to hide or show specific panels on your admin screen to reduce clutter for clients.

Content Management vs Dev: What is the difference between a Web Developer and a Content Manager? Developers build the architecture, themes, and logic; Content Managers use the dashboard to publish daily text and images without touching code!

Writing Content: The Block Editor

What is the modern default WordPress editor called? The Block Editor (Gutenberg), named after the inventor of the printing press. How does the Block Editor handle content? Every paragraph, image, or video is its own movable block.

Mastering the Editor
  • Adding Content: Where do you find the button to add new elements in the Block Editor? Look for The '+' Icon (the universal symbol for add/new).
  • Moving Content: What feature in the Block Editor allows you to move a paragraph above an image? The drag-and-drop handles or up/down arrows on the block.
  • Custom Code: If the Block Editor doesn't have a specific visual feature you need, how can you write code directly? Use a 'Custom HTML Block'!
  • Mistakes: If 'Manager Mike' accidentally deletes a paragraph in the Block Editor, what is the fastest way to get it back? Use the 'Undo' button (or Ctrl+Z) in the editor.
  • Revisions: If you really mess up, you can restore a backup. What is a WordPress 'Revision'? An automatically saved previous version of a post that you can restore if you make a mistake.
Featured Images & Shortcodes

Every post needs a Featured Image. What is it? The main thumbnail people see when the post is shared on social media.

You will also frequently use Shortcodes. What is a 'Shortcode'? A small piece of code in brackets (e.g., [gallery id="5"]) that executes a larger script or plugin feature within a post.

The Clothes: Themes & Child Themes

What controls the 'Look' or visual design of a WordPress site? A Theme. This dictates your global colors, typography, and layout structure. If you want to build a highly customized page layout for a client that the standard Block Editor can't handle, what type of plugin would you install? A Page Builder Plugin (like Elementor or Divi).

Widgets & Menus

Themes give you control over sidebars and footers using Widgets. What is a 'Widget' in the context of a CMS? A small block that performs a specific function, often placed in sidebars or footers (like a search bar or calendar). Where would you typically place a copyright notice that needs to appear on every single page of the site? In the Footer (via Widgets or Theme Customizer).

Navigation is handled separately. Where do you manage the layout of your site's navigation links? Appearance > Menus.

The Golden Rule: Child Themes

What is the primary purpose of a Child Theme? To ensure custom code isn't deleted during theme updates. If you write custom CSS into a parent theme, and the developer pushes an update, your code will be wiped out! A child theme safely inherits the parent's design while protecting the agency's custom coding.

Plugins, Security, & SEO

In the 'Smartphone' analogy, what do WordPress Plugins represent? The apps you download to give the phone new powers. What is used to add new functionality (like a store or contact form) to a WordPress site? A Plugin. (For example, what is 'WooCommerce'? The most popular e-commerce plugin that turns a WordPress site into an online store!)

The Danger of Plugin Bloat

What is the primary risk of installing too many WordPress plugins? They can slow down the site, cause conflicts, and create security vulnerabilities. This is known as "Plugin Bloat."

Furthermore, what does it mean if a plugin has not been 'tested with your current version of WordPress'? It might be abandoned by its developer and could break your site or introduce security flaws. Updating vulnerable plugins immediately demonstrates the WRS skill of Probity / Ethical Adherence.

Security & Roles

How do you ensure a client's WordPress site remains secure from hackers? Regularly update the WordPress core, themes, and plugins to patch vulnerabilities. BUT wait! Before updating the WordPress core or installing a major plugin, what is the single most important action a professional developer must take? Perform a complete database and file Backup.

Why is it important to assign 'Roles' (like Editor, Author, Contributor) to users in a CMS? To restrict permissions so an author (like Manager Mike) can't accidentally delete the entire website or change the theme. Which user role has absolute total control over every aspect of a WordPress site? The Administrator.

SEO & Caching

How does a CMS help with SEO (Search Engine Optimization)? By automatically generating clean HTML structure, sitemaps, and allowing plugins (like Yoast) to optimize keywords.

To make your site lightning fast, use Caching plugins. What is 'Caching' in the context of a CMS? Creating static copies of dynamic pages so they load much faster for visitors.
