THE RULES

Architecture, Law & Ethics for Professional Developers

1. Intellectual Property (IP) Shields

COPYRIGHT ©
Automatic Shield

Protects original code, text, and art the second they are fixed. Work for Hire means your boss owns your office output.

TRADEMARK ™
Brand Sentry

Protects brand identity (logos, slogans). Prevents market confusion and Infringement.

PATENT ℗
Invention Ledger

Protects how something works for 20 years. Not automatic—you must apply to the gov.

Fair Use Factor: A limited defense for education/news. Factor check: Purpose, Nature, Amount, and Market Effect.

2. The Secure Handshake (HTTPS)

HTTP is a Postcard. HTTPS is a Sealed Letter. It uses an 8-step greeting sequence.

1. HELLO
2. CERTIFICATE
3. VERIFY (CA)
4. SESSION KEY
5. ENCRYPT
6. UNLOCK
7. SHARED CODE
8. SECURE!
Password Protocol:

Hashing: Irreversible fingerprint. Salting: Adding random noise to stop Rainbow Table hacks.

3. Global Privacy Laws

GDPR (Europe) - OPT-IN

You must ask for specific consent before collecting any PII. Includes the Right to be Forgotten.

SB220 (Nevada) - OPT-OUT

Requires a clear link to "Stop Selling My Data." Focused on data sales protection.

Professional Ethic: Privacy by Design

Building protection into the logic from day one, not as an afterthought.

Digital Footprint: The permanent, searchable internet tattoo of your activity.

4. Accessibility (POUR)

The ADA requires sites to follow WCAG Level AA.

  • 🔵 P - Perceivable: Can users see/hear it? (Alt Text).
  • 🔵 O - Operable: Can users use a keyboard? (Focus Rings).
  • 🔵 U - Understandable: Is the interface confusing?
  • 🔵 R - Robust: Does it work on Screen Readers?

The Curb Cut Effect

Designing for marginalized users (the blind) makes things better for everyone (e.g., captions help in a loud room; high contrast helps in sunlight).

Contrast Rule: 4.5:1 for normal text.

5. The NDA Protocol

A legally binding contract protecting Trade Secrets (Proprietary code logic).

Survival Clause: Secrets stay secret after you leave.
Indefinite: Unlike patents, secrets don't expire if kept quiet.
