THE "WHO" BLUEPRINT

Empathy, Personas, and the Laws of User Experience

1. The Anatomy of Understanding

We use User Research to stop designing for ourselves and start designing with Empathy.

Demographics (The What)

Objective, measurable traits: Age, Gender, Location, Income.

Psychographics (The Why)

Internal motivations: Values, Attitudes, Interests, Personality.

Research Methods:
  • 📋 Qualitative: Interviews to find the emotional "Why" and Pain Points.
  • 📊 Quantitative: Surveys to prove the "How Many" with statistics.

3. The Laws of Efficiency

The 80/20 Rule

80% of users only use 20% of your features. Action: Find that 20% and make it the main focus.

Curb Cut Effect

Designing for marginalized users (blind/mobile) helps everyone. Part of Universal Design.

Voice & Tone:

The "Personality" of your site. It must match your Persona (e.g., Fun for Penny, Professional for Sam).

4. The Architect's Checklist

  • 🛡️ Inclusive Representation: Do your photos and content represent diverse backgrounds and abilities?
  • 🚀 Transparent Technology: Is the UX so smooth that the technology becomes "invisible" to the user?
  • 👣 User Journey: The chronological sequence of steps a user takes to achieve their goal.
  • ✅ Success Metric: The measurable proof that the user successfully completed their specific task.
User Story Format:
As a [Persona], I want to [Action] so that I can [Goal].
