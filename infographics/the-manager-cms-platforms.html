The Content Manager

Rental vs. Ownership

A Content Manager must choose how much control they need. Businesses almost always choose the Owner model for total freedom.

WordPress.com (The Rental):

Host manages security and updates. You are limited by their rules. Harder to customize.

WordPress.org (The Owner):

Open-Source software you host yourself. Total control over code, plugins, and monetization.

Themes vs. Plugins

Content and design are separate. One database can wear many "outfits."

THE CLOTHES
Theme

Controls the visual "Look" (CSS/Layout). Swapping a theme changes the design but never deletes your text or photos.

THE APPS
Plugin

Adds new "Powers" (Functionality). Examples: WooCommerce (Store), Yoast (SEO), or Contact Forms. Warning: Too many can slow the site down.

Posts vs. Pages

POSTS (Timely)
  • News, blog updates, or sales.
  • Reverse-chronological order.
  • Uses Categories & Tags.
  • Appears in RSS feeds.
PAGES (Timeless)
  • About Us, Contact, or Policies.
  • Static and permanent.
  • Lives in Navigation Menus.
  • Usually does not use tags.

The Manager's Protocols

  • Backups: The Golden Rule. Perform a full backup before any update.
  • Roles: Assign permissions (Subscriber to Administrator) to protect site settings.
  • SEO: Use Permalinks for readable URLs (/news vs /?p=123).
  • Caching: Speed up the site by serving static copies of dynamic pages.
Vendor Lock-in: A risk of Wix/Squarespace where you don't own your code and cannot easily move your data to a new host. Solution: WordPress.org.
