1. Choosing Your Format

📸
JPG / WebP

Best for: Photos.
Key: High compression, "Lossy" data removal.

🎨
PNG

Best for: Graphics.
Key: Transparency support, "Lossless" quality.

📐
SVG

Best for: Logos.
Key: Code-based, vector, infinitely sharp.

🎞️
GIF

Best for: Memes.
Key: Supports simple looped animation.

Raster (Pixels)

Made of fixed dots. If you zoom in, they get blurry or "pixelated." (JPG, PNG, GIF)

Vector (Math)

Made of mathematical paths. They stay perfectly sharp no matter how big you scale them. (SVG)

2. The Optimization Funnel

Turning 5MB Bricks into 150KB Feathers

1. CROP (C)

Remove dead space. Focus on Hobbyist Harry or the Drone. Don't waste bandwidth on empty sky.

2. RESIZE (Alt+I)

Web images rarely need to be wider than 1200px. Use 'Bicubic Sharper' for reductions.

3. COMPRESS

Save for Web (Legacy). Aim for Quality 60-80. Target file size: Under 200KB.
