Web Design I, II, & AS | Instructor: Ms. Miller

Welcome to the Studio

When you step through the doors of this classroom, you are no longer just a student—you are a Junior Developer and UI/UX Designer. This space operates as a professional digital arts studio. Our expectations reflect the standards of the modern tech industry: we value creativity, respect, collaboration, and extreme ownership of your work.

The Employee Handbook

1. The Professional Workspace (Hardware Care)
  • Hardware Respect: You are working on expensive, professional-grade equipment. Treat the Macs/PCs, monitors, keyboards, and mice with extreme care.
  • Food & Drink: Keep all food and open liquids completely off the computer desks. Closed water bottles must remain on the floor to prevent catastrophic spills.
  • End of Shift: Before you "clock out" (the bell rings), log out of your accounts, close your applications, push in your chair, and throw away any trash.
2. Time Management & Deadlines
  • Clocking In: Be in your seat and logged into your workstation when the bell rings. Missing the "Daily Stand-up" (instruction time) will leave you behind.
  • Client Deadlines: In the real world, missing a client deadline means losing the contract. Late work will lose points. If you need an extension, you must communicate with the "Project Manager" (Ms. Miller) before the deadline.
  • Distractions: Cell phones should be silenced and put away during instructional time. During independent lab time, headphones are allowed.
3. The Code of Ethics & AI Policy
  • Collaboration vs. Plagiarism: Helping a peer debug a broken CSS layout is highly encouraged. Emailing them your completed HTML file to copy and paste is plagiarism.
  • AI Assistants (ChatGPT, etc.): Generative AI is a powerful tool for brainstorming or generating placeholder text/images. However, you may never use AI to write the core logic of your assignments.
  • Open Source Rules: If you use a snippet of code, a custom font, or an image from the internet, you must properly attribute it in your comments or footer.
4. Professional Communication
  • The "Three Then Me" Rule: When you get stuck on a coding bug, consult three resources (Google, your digital notebook, or a peer) before asking the instructor. This builds independent problem-solving skills.
  • Respect the Studio: We are a diverse guild of creators. Harassment, offensive language, or disrespecting another developer's work will result in immediate "termination" from the network.
